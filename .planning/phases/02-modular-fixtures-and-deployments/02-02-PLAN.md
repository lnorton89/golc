## Phase Goal

**As a** show author, **I want to** validate fixtures and adapt logical pools to concrete deployments through atomic impact review, **so that** one show adapts to different rigs without being rebuilt.

**This plan's slice:** A show author can run `golc fixture inspect <file.yaml>` and see the fixture's stable identity, content hash, schema version, revision, validation result, and provenance/warnings before committing it to a show — so a later library update cannot silently change an existing show.

<objective>
Give every canonical FixtureDefinition a content-addressed identity (FIXT-05) and a provenance record (FIXT-06), and expose both through a new `golc fixture inspect <file>` route. Identity/hash pinning reuses the exact `internal/strictjson.CanonicalEncode` + `crypto/sha256` pattern already proven by `internal/trace/apply/guard.go` (no bespoke hash scheme — RESEARCH Don't-Hand-Roll). Provenance carries source, schema version, content hash, revision, validation result, and the lossy/unsupported-warning list that 02-03's OFL import will populate.

Purpose: FIXT-05 pinning is the mechanism that makes a show reproducible offline after import (D-07); FIXT-06 inspection is how an author reviews a fixture's trustworthiness before use.
Output: `internal/fixture/{identity,provenance}.go`, tests, and the `fixture inspect` route added to `internal/command/fixture.go`.
</objective>
