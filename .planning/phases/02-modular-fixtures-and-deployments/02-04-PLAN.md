## Phase Goal

**As a** show author, **I want to** validate fixtures and adapt logical pools to concrete deployments through atomic impact review, **so that** one show adapts to different rigs without being rebuilt.

**This plan's slice:** A show author can run `golc pool create <name>`, `golc deployment create <name>`, activate a deployment, and `golc show inspect` to see the logical pools and deployments — the walking skeleton of the pool/deployment thread, operating on a working ShowState document.

<objective>
Establish the pool/deployment domain model and the `ShowState` substrate the rest of Thread B mutates. A `Pool` (POOL-01) is a named logical grouping of compatible fixtures defined independently of count/address/hardware; a `Deployment` (POOL-02, D-09) is a named mapping of pool members to concrete `Instance`s (mode/universe/address), with one deployment active at a time; a `Group` (D-10) is a cross-pool named selection the impact walk will later treat as a dependent. All live in a `ShowState` JSON document (a working representation Phase 5 will later supersede with the durable `.golc` format) carrying a monotonic `Revision` that 02-05's freshness guard checks (D-16). Pool/deployment operations self-register through `internal/command` (D-04). Scale target is small-rig (~10-50 fixtures, 3-8 pools — D-12); operations are synchronous and simple.

Purpose: This is the domain substrate the impact-review engine (02-05) and fixture substitution (02-06) operate on. Without stable pool/deployment/instance identities and a revisioned ShowState, there is nothing for an atomic reviewed apply to mutate.
Output: `internal/show/state.go`, `internal/pool/model.go`, `internal/deployment/model.go`, `internal/command/{pool,deployment}.go`, tests, and the `google/uuid` dependency.
</objective>
