## Phase Goal

**As an** Art-Net operator, **I want to** drive a small lighting rig with correct Art-Net 4 frames computed from the deterministic playback engine and independently verify protocol, target, and timing health, **so that** I can run reliable live output without needing the desktop UI.

> Derived from the ROADMAP.md Phase 4 `**Goal:**` line ("Operators can drive a small Art-Net rig from deterministic complete frames and verify protocol, target, and timing health independently of the desktop UI"), which is prose rather than canonical user-story form. All three story slots are present in that goal, so this is a restatement, not an invention. If the team wants the ROADMAP goal line itself normalized, run `/gsd mvp-phase 4`.

<objective>
Establish the pure, unit-testable Art-Net encoding foundation: the additive per-mode DMX channel-order data model (D-16/D-17), the byte-exact ArtDMX packet encoder, and the semantic-frame-to-DMX-bytes channel map. After this plan a Frame's semantic values can be turned into the exact DMX bytes and the exact ArtDMX packet that would go on the wire — verifiable against golden byte vectors — even though nothing is sent yet.

Purpose: Every later slice (worker, daemon, CLI) depends on correct bytes. Getting addressing, sequencing, payload length, and the channel mapping provably correct first is what makes the on-wire slice (Plan 03) trustworthy.
Output: `internal/artnet` package seeded with `packet.go` + `channelmap.go`; `fixture.Mode` gains its channel layout; `fixture` decode enforces D-17.

Assumption-delta note (advisory, pre-resolved): this plan introduces a second data axis onto the previously singular `fixture.Mode` (channel order) and a second addressing representation (Art-Net Port-Address alongside the flat `Instance.Universe`). Per CONTEXT.md D-16 the channel-order axis is added additively to the one canonical `fixture.Mode`, not a competing side model — this checkpoint is pre-resolved and is recorded here rather than re-litigated.
</objective>
